    Write Winning Upwork Proposals:

    5 Tips to Stand Out 🚀

    With intense competition 🤼‍♂️ on Upwork, crafting the right proposal has never been more critical. Regardless of your JSS, history, feedback, or profile, your proposal is your golden opportunity to turn the game in your favor. On the flip side, a poorly written proposal can be the biggest turn-off 😒 for potential clients.

    ‘95 out of 100 proposals are trash 🗑️’

    To ensure you’re in the top 5% 🥇 of outstanding proposal writers, follow these five proven tips that will help you stand out and win more projects.

    1. Use the Client’s Name

    Hearing one’s name creates excitement, fosters trust, and grabs attention. Starting your proposal with the client’s name increases the likelihood they’ll read through it entirely.

    But how do you find the client’s name?

    📌 Here are three reliable ways:

    • Check the Job Description : Many clients include their name at the start or end of the job description.

    • Research : If the client mentions a company name, website, or other details, research these to find their name on Google or social media.

    • Read Feedback : Look at the client’s past reviews. Freelancers often mention the client’s name in their feedback.

    💡 Example

    "Hi [Client's Name], I noticed your project focuses on [specific details]. Here's how I can help..."

    2. Kick the ‘I’ Out of Your Proposal

    Hearing one’s name creates excitement, fosters trust, and grabs attention. Starting your proposal with the client’s name increases the likelihood they’ll read through it entirely.

    But how do you find the client’s name?

    Avoid starting with:

    • I liked your job.

    • I want to work on your project.

    • I have X years of experience.

    💡 Example

    Instead of saying, “I have 5 years of experience in marketing,” try: “Your project to boost brand visibility aligns perfectly with my proven strategies for increasing engagement and driving results.”

    3. Show, Don’t Just Say

    In a competitive environment 🥊, clients appreciate freelancers who save them time ⏳ by offering practical solutions.

    🔑 Instead of vague claims like “I have experience in this field,” provide tangible examples:

    • Share similar projects you’ve completed.

    • Offer actionable suggestions related to their project.

    Pro Tip :

    Demonstrate how you’ll solve their problem within the proposal itself to build trust and credibility.

    4. Build a Connection 🤝

    Clients want to work with someone they feel connected to. Create a sense of familiarity by highlighting common ground—be it emotional, geographic, or even linguistic.

    💡 Example :

    Mirror their tone and language. If the client’s description is friendly and casual, match their style.

    5. End with a Strong CTA 💥

    The end of your proposal is just as crucial as the start. Use a Call-to-Action (CTA) to guide the client to the next step.

    For instance:

    • Ask a simple question to prompt a response.

    • Suggest a call to discuss the project further.

    💡 Example CTA:

    “Do you have any questions about my approach? I’d be happy to discuss this over a quick call to explore how I can help you achieve your goals.”
